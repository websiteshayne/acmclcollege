Arc of The Covenant Montessori Chamber of Learning

ACMCL COLLEGE An Institution of Excellence The concept of putting up a school was envisioned in a DREAM by Dr. Estrellita A. Dolor, THE FOUNDRES. With her passion to TEACH and to SERVE the YOUTH, this genuine DREAM turned into a REALITY: the birth of Ark of the Covenant Montessori Chamber of Learning. June 13, 1993 was the first day of operation of the ACMCL, which offered 3-levels of Pre-Elementary Course (CASA, JUNIOR CASA and ADVANCE CASA), three teachers (Tchr. Ghie Hernandez, Tchr. Marieta Bayeta and Tchr. Aida Ortega), one teacher aide (Tchr. Nancy Castillo), a service jeepney, and a 9x7 Multipurpose building to house the 65 enrollees. A Thanksgiving Mass celebrated by Rev. Fr. Jose San Juan, Jr. SVD highlighted the first Montessori Week of ACMCL in August 1993, the birth month of Maria Montessori. Ms. Cherry Rose Anne "Cheche" Bautista and Mr. Albert Marcelo gave color to the celebration as the first Mr. & Ms. Little AC Montessori. ACMCL, at the very start, has been provided with very participative and understanding parents, with Dr. Baldomero Esteban as the first PTA President. The first twenty-four (24) Graduates of Advance Casa marched on March 30, 1994 and were confirmed by Tito Tony H. Dolor, the first DIRECTOR of ACMCL with the Schools Division Superintendent of Oriental Mindoro Dr. Rebecca V. de Ocampo as the Guest of Honor. The lone 9x7 meter building had a partner. A 9-room building was constructed to house the Elementary pupils in S.Y. 1994-1995 from 65 to 185 enrollees and from 3 to 11 teachers. In 1995-1996 another 4-room building was constructed getting ready for the offering of the secondary course and post-secondary courses. It was in 1997-1998 when ACMCL witnessed the first Graduates of Computer Programming and Office Management marched triumphantly on stage. The first Dance Workshop was successfully done in the same year with Teacher Nonoy Brucal, a Bayanihan Dance Troupe Member as Instructor where pupils learned sixty national and international dances. This developed the Montessorians into skillful ballet and folkdancers. On the other hand, the first Junior and Senior Prom was held on February 14, 1999 at Bahay Tuklasan, Naujan. Tertiary Courses like Bachelor of Secondary Education with English and Mathematics as field of Specialization and Bachelor of Elementary Education were offered in 2002. Consequently the name of the school was changed from Ark of the Covenant Montessori Chamber of Learning to ACMCL College. ACMCL's existence continued to earn the genuine trust of the public. Enrolment remarkably increased in all levels. Additional buildings were constructed. Physical facilities were upgraded. Teachers regularly underwent trainings and seminars for professional development. As a result, ACMCL College received a PLAQUE OF RECOGNITION from Director Paraluman Giron of Region IV dated July 20, 2001 for Ark of the Covenant Montessori Chamber of Learning as UPPER 10% of the COUNTRY'S National Elementary Achievement Test Result of 2000. The second School Director was appointed by the ACMCL Board of Trustees in the person of Dr. Humerlito A. Dolor, (now the Governor of Oriental Mindoro) in June 2002 with Tchr. Norman F. Magsino as the Principal of the High School Department and Engr. Humphrey A. Dolor as Head of Student Affairs and School Administrative Officer at the same time. The first 13 students of the Bachelor of Secondary Education under the Deanship of Dr. Rosita T. Zamora were conferred their degrees by the College President Tito Tony H. Dolor. In S.Y. 2011-2012, there was a change in leadership. Engr. Humphrey A. Dolor was appointed as the new College Director. In the same school year, Special Science Class was opened. Improvement of Multi-Media Facilities and Installation of CCTV were done. ACMCL College was made a Wifi campus, with website www.acmclcollege.com which features e-lessons and online journal which were officially launched on September 19, 2012. It was also during this time when Engr. Humphrey first conducted the STRONG seminar, a value-laden training in Villa Bautista Resort and has become a regular PROGRAM for Values Education. It emphasizes the virtues of being, STEADFAST, TRUSTWORTHY, RESPECTFUL, OPEN-MINDED, NOBLE and GUTSY. This was patterned from the University of Asia and the Pacific. Through the years, ACMCL College has been consistent in her QUEST for EXCELLENCE. Quality Education through competent and committed teachers, efficient leaders, complete and modernized facilities, to devel- op and challenge every young mind who enters her portals ensuring to make a difference in their lives. This continues to be ACMCL College guiding principle. PADAYON HAPPY MONTESSORIANS.
